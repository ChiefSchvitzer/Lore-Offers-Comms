How to ensure discounted sessions are used this weekend
Recommended
Buy and book simultaneously
Don't issue a floating credit — require customers to select a specific time slot at the point of purchase. The discount is tied to the reservation, not a redeemable credit. No slot selected, no discount unlocked. The message writes itself: "Choose your session time to lock in this price."
Recommended
Hard expiry at Sunday midnight
Any discounted session credit purchased this weekend expires Sunday at midnight. Make this front and center in the offer — not buried in fine print. The threat of losing the deal is a stronger motivator than the deal itself.
Idea
48-hour expiry from time of purchase
Credit expires 48 hours from when it was bought. Naturally compresses usage into the weekend without a fixed date per customer. Slightly harder to administer but feels more dynamic.
Idea
Limited slots, not limited time
Cap the offer at a specific number of redemptions — "only 20 spots at this price this weekend." Scarcity of supply drives action faster than scarcity of time. Works especially well in an SMS or email subject line.
Idea
Flash purchase window
The offer is only purchasable within a short window (e.g. Friday 6pm to Saturday noon). The session can be used anytime that weekend, but the ability to buy the discount closes early. Creates a burst of purchase activity and commits people to showing up.
