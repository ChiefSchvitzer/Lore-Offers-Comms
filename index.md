## How to Ensure Discounted Sessions Are Used This Weekend

### ✅ Recommended: Buy and Book Simultaneously
Don't issue a floating credit — require customers to select a specific time slot at the point of purchase. The discount is tied to the reservation, not a redeemable credit. No slot selected, no discount unlocked. The message writes itself: *"Choose your session time to lock in this price."*

### ✅ Recommended: Hard Expiry at Sunday Midnight
Any discounted session credit purchased this weekend expires Sunday at midnight. Make this front and center in the offer — not buried in fine print. The threat of losing the deal is a stronger motivator than the deal itself.

### 💡 Idea: 48-Hour Expiry from Time of Purchase
Credit expires 48 hours from when it was bought. Naturally compresses usage into the weekend without needing a fixed date per customer. Slightly harder to administer but feels more dynamic.

### 💡 Idea: Limited Slots, Not Limited Time
Cap the offer at a specific number of redemptions — *"only 20 spots at this price this weekend."* Scarcity of supply drives action faster than scarcity of time.

### 💡 Idea: Flash Purchase Window
The discounted offer is only purchasable within a short window (e.g. Friday 6pm to Saturday noon). The session itself can be used anytime that weekend, but the ability to buy the discount closes early.
